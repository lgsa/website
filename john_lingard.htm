Sad News:  John Lingard has died.  He was 90 on 19th May this year and made his usual inspiring visit to the 2005 reunion in the afternoon at Liskeard Public Hall.  The funeral was private, for family only and at present there has been no indication of a Memorial Service.  Members may wish to add a tribute to John Lingard - anecdotes would be very welcome.  The photo is Sports Day 1961 with Head of Pendennis House (Current LGSA chairman Peter Jay) holding the cup.

Patrick Coulter (1959) Like so many of us, John Lingard touched my life. I recall his example, his true modesty, his high personal standards and his astounding ability to be fair. Even when standing before him, guilty as sin, in the absence of evidence, you were innocent and walked free. Well, perhaps not free, because you took with you the knowledge that both parties knew you were guilty. but you, the lesser being, did not have the moral fibre to own-up, whilst he had administered true justice - and a lesson for life.
The next time, you owned-up.
One of my most valued possessions is a reference from him: a document that I did not have the maturity to appreciate at nineteen, but now, some forty years on, I can appreciate - the combination of kindness, honesty and perception. Despite what we may have thought of him at the time - a determination to ensure that everyone achieved their academic best - his leadership was about so much more; it was about helping us to be decent people.
He & I did have our run-ins and he always saw fit - in my case, at least - not to resort to the cane. At the end of my leaving interview, I remarked on this. He gave me that slow smile with his eyes half closed and feigned surprise. Now I know what he was really thinking - thanks to him.
Jean Brand (Northcott) (1946) During my last year at LCGS I very vividly remember the horrible ordeal of having to read the lesson for a whole week at a time.  You had to choose a passage from the Bible to fit in with that day's hymn. Unfortunately, one morning the markers fell out, so I just read from the first page that fell open. This fortuitously didn't really matter as on one morning of that week Mr Lingard said to me "Although I was standing next to you I couldn't hear a word" That lunchtime I found myself in the Hall standing at the lectern reading "aloud" to him, much to the amusement of 2 or 3 boys in my form.  Now my husband - but maybe he is a trifle deaf - says he doesn't hear me!
Tony Stephens (1946) I am convinced that the current success of the LGSA is very much thanks to Mr Lingards influence on the School . Before his arrival , Liskeard Grammar was Dickensian in character . Learning was by rote and it was not until his arrival that we, the pupils, suddenly realised that education could be enjoyable . So many thousands of former pupils have cause to be grateful for his enthusiasm, dedication and love of his chosen profession and he will be sorely missed as mentor, friend and example
Denis Congdon (1953-1960) A real gentleman. He gave the pupils of LGS a good grounding for life ahead although we probably didn't appreciate it at the time. He had a tremendous influence on my life.
Peter Jay (1955) At the 2000 reunion John Lingard referred to his idea of providing a "liberal" education at LGS - I think he was right! A pity there are not a few more like him around today.
Maggie (Humphrey) Willan (1946-1953) A role model for all who were fortunate enough to know him.  I shall miss his kindness, interest, support and enthusiasm - qualities which never left him.
Do (Hannington) Bagley (1949-1956) Mr Lingard came to the school when I was, I think, in the 3rd form.  He was always ready to listen to ideas from pupils and was a most courteous, patient and kind gentleman - an excellent example to his pupils.
Roger Bennett (1953-1960) John Lingard (Jimmy to us) was a lovely man. Strict and fair. I lived in awe of him at school. In the Upper Sixth, I used my status as Head Boy to protest with Swannie against doing P.E. We were told off but let off!. After coming back to Cornwall in 1987, I visited him several times. He was so interested in my activities, and very generous with the plants he often gave me. Not only did he serve me well at school, but he still does in my garden!
